## The problem

Long-form video is the dominant content format online — recorded lectures, meetings,
news, sports — but humans rarely have time to watch it end to end. A summary that
captures the *informative* moments is far more useful than a fixed-length compression.

The challenge: pick the few keyframes that preserve the story, not just visual variety.

## The pipeline

The full stack lives in
[`chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso)
(MIT licensed). End-to-end it goes:

1. **Shot segmentation** — KTS (Kernel Temporal Segmentation, [`kts.py`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso/blob/main/kts.py)) breaks the input into change-point shots.
2. **Feature extraction** — A ResNet ([`feature_extraction.py`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso/blob/main/feature_extraction.py)) produces a dense embedding per frame.
3. **Encoder–decoder** — A BiLSTM encoder consumes the embeddings; the decoder uses either **Bahdanau** ([`bahdanau_attention.py`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso/blob/main/bahdanau_attention.py)) or **Luong** ([`luong_attention.py`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso/blob/main/luong_attention.py)) attention to score frame importance.
4. **Selection** — A 0/1 knapsack ([`knapsack.py`](https://github.com/chandan181singh/video-summarizer-using-encorder-decoder-and-pso/blob/main/knapsack.py)) picks the highest-value shots that fit a length budget.
5. **Hyperparameter search** — Particle Swarm Optimization tunes LSTM hidden size, attention dropout, and learning rate to maximize F-score.

```python
# Luong (dot) attention — sketch
scores = decoder_h @ encoder_outputs.transpose(-1, -2)   # (B, T_dec, T_enc)
weights = softmax(scores, dim=-1)
context = weights @ encoder_outputs                       # (B, T_dec, H)
```

## Why two attentions?

Bahdanau (additive) and Luong (multiplicative) make different speed/quality
trade-offs. Comparing them on the same encoder lets me pick a winner per dataset
instead of trusting whatever the paper-of-the-week says.

## What worked

- **Bidirectional context** boosted F-score noticeably over a vanilla LSTM — the past
  *and* future of a frame both matter when ranking it.
- **Knapsack-based selection** beats greedy top-k by respecting shot lengths, so the
  final summary fits the target duration exactly.
- **PSO over grid search** converged faster on a usable hyperparameter region with
  less compute, especially for the dropout/learning-rate combo.
- **KTS pre-segmentation** kept training tractable on long inputs — instead of feeding
  thousands of frames, the model sees compressed shot-level sequences.

## What I learned

- Attention is not magic; the *features* you attend over matter more.
- Reproducibility of ML research is hard — pinning seeds, dataset splits, and ResNet
  pre-trained weights was half the battle.
- A clean evaluation harness pays back tenfold when iterating on architectures.
